Stereo Records

Stereo photography was the VR of the 1950s.

In 2021, I captured these film-based stereograms of Vancouver's thriving record stores.

The Kodak Stereo camera was produced in the same era (1954 to 1959) as the first commercially available stereo records (starting in 1958). I used discontinued, and long expired, Fujichrome MS100/1000 film exposed at the fast speed of ISO 1000 which explains the distorted colours and ample grain.

Experience the autostereogram 3D effect by crossing your eyes so the left eye sees the right-hand image while the right eye sees the left-hand image.

Start with a small screen or browser window or increase the viewing distance, then cross your eyes until you see three images as the two images merge in the middle. You brain's visual cortex will do the rest.

It can be helpful to blink while attempting to focus.
