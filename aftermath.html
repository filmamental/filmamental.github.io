Aftermath

The Kingsway-Joyce 7-11, in East Vancouver, closed at the beginning of March, 40 years after it opened in 1983. A dark grainy photo from the Vancouver Archives appears to be from the mid-1980s when there were gas pumps in front of the store and gasoline was 42 cents per litre. My photos here show what remained after the signs came down and the lights went out in 2023.

It probably doesn’t require a lot of math by accountants to determine that a 13,520 square foot piece of commercial property assessed at $8.5 million is not maximizing revenue as a convenience store. No surprise either that the city’s zoning change report is very positive about recommending development of a new six story rental apartment building without bemoaning the loss of convenient snacks and Slurpees on a hot summer night.

On the opposite side of Joyce Street, the century-old Sir Guy Carleton school has been kept in tact but vacant, in wait of needed renovations. There is no way a garish 40 year old privately owned chain store, even if it is one of the finest examples of 1980s convenience store design, could ever compete for heritage status with an historic school property but it is a loss felt by some none the less.

I hope these photos, and especially my HEAVEN ELEVEN series, in some small measure preserve the memory of this now departed establishment.

Vancouver, May 2023
