Stereo Records

I captured these film-based stereograms of Vancouver's thriving record stores in 2021.

Stereo photography in colour was the 3D TV of the 1950s.

The Kodak Stereo camera was produced in the same era (1954 to 1959) as the first commercially available stereo records (starting in 1958). Kodachrome slide film was the film of choice to produce a vivid colour stereo pair mounted for viewing with a stereoscope. I used Fujichrome MS100/1000 film, discontinued a decade ago or more, exposed at the fast speed of ISO 1000 which explains the distorted colours and ample grain.

Experience the autostereogram 3D effect by crossing your eyes so the left eye sees the right-hand image while the right eye sees the left-hand image.

Start with a small screen or browser window or increase the viewing distance, then cross your eyes until you see three images as the two images merge in the middle. You brain's visual cortex will do the rest.

It can be helpful to blink while attempting to focus.
